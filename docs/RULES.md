# How to Play

Mafia is a social deduction game. A few players are secretly evil; everyone
else is trying to figure out who. The game alternates between **night**
(secret actions) and **day** (open discussion and voting) until one side wins.

You'll want a voice call (Discord works great) alongside the game — the game
handles roles, timers, and votes; the arguing is up to you.

Every rule below is also available in-game: tap the **? Rules** button in the
corner at any time, on any screen. It only shows the roles that are actually
in your current game.

---

## The Flow of a Round

1. **Night** — Players with night powers secretly pick targets (kill, protect,
   investigate…). Everyone else waits. Night ends when the timer runs out or
   everyone has acted.
2. **Morning** — The game announces what happened: who died, who was saved.
3. **Discussion** — Talk on your voice call. Who's acting weird? The host can
   end discussion early.
4. **Voting** — Everyone votes to execute one player, or votes Skip. Votes
   are public. A tie, or Skip winning, means no execution.
5. Repeat until one side wins.

**Dead players** stay in the room and watch everything, but can't act, vote,
or talk in chat. House rule you must enforce yourselves: the dead stay silent
on the voice call — no hints!

---

## The Three Sides

- **☀️ Town** — the majority. Wins when every Mafia member *and* the Serial
  Killer are dead.
- **🔪 Mafia** — a hidden team. They know each other and share a private night
  chat. Wins when the Mafia matches or outnumbers everyone else (at that point
  they control every vote).
- **🎭 Neutral** — loners with personal goals. They win (or lose) on their own
  terms.

---

## Town Roles

### 🧑‍🌾 Villager
No powers. Your weapons are your ears, your logic, and your vote.

### 💉 Doctor
Each night, protect one player (yourself included). If they're attacked that
night, they quietly survive — the town only hears "someone was saved."

### 🕵️ Detective
Each night, investigate one player. You learn **SUSPICIOUS** (Mafia or Serial
Killer) or **INNOCENT**. One catch: the **Godfather shows up as INNOCENT**.

### 🛡️ Bodyguard
Each night, guard another player. If they're attacked, **you die in their
place** — loudly. Everyone hears that someone died protecting someone.
If a Doctor protected *you* that night, you survive the fight.
The difference from the Doctor: the Doctor's save is silent and safe; yours
costs your life and makes noise.

### 🔫 Vigilante
You carry **one bullet** for the whole game. On any night, shoot one player —
or hold fire. But if you kill a Town member, the guilt kills you the following
night, and nothing can prevent it. (Killing a Neutral doesn't trigger guilt —
only innocent townsfolk.)

### 🏛️ Mayor
Once per game, at any point during a day phase, you may **reveal yourself as
the Mayor**. From then on your vote counts as **two votes** — but everyone,
including the Mafia, now knows exactly who you are. Expect a knife with your
name on it. You can't un-reveal.

---

## Mafia Roles

### 🕶️ Mafia
You know your teammates and share a private chat that opens at night. Each
night, the team votes on one victim — the target with the most Mafia votes
dies (ties are a coin flip). During the day: lie, deflect, survive.

### 🎩 Godfather
The boss. You join the nightly kill vote like everyone else, but Detective
investigations report you as **INNOCENT**. Regular Mafia members still show as
Suspicious — you're the disguise, not them.

### 📜 Consigliere
The Mafia's own investigator. Each night, learn one player's **exact role**
(not just their alignment) — far more precise than the Detective. If every
Mafia killer is dead, you pick up the gun and choose the night kill yourself.

---

## Neutral Roles

### 🃏 Jester
You win **alone** if the town votes to execute you during the day. Dying at
night counts for nothing. Act suspicious — but not so obviously that people
smell the act. When the Jester is executed, the game ends immediately with a
Jester victory.

### 🗡️ Serial Killer
You kill one player every night, and you answer to no one — the Mafia is as
much your enemy as the Town. You show up as Suspicious to the Detective. You
win if you survive until at most one other player remains. In a final
standoff, the Serial Killer wins.

### 🌱 Survivor
No powers, no side. You win if you're alive when the game ends — alongside
whoever else wins. Your enemy is attention.

---

## Night Order (who resolves first)

When several things happen in the same night, they resolve in this order:

1. **Guilt** — a Vigilante who shot a Town member the previous night dies.
   Cannot be prevented.
2. **Protection locks in** — Doctor heals and Bodyguard watches.
3. **Attacks land**, in order: **Mafia → Serial Killer → Vigilante.**
   Each attack checks, in order:
   - Is the target already dead tonight? The attack is wasted.
   - Is the target guarded? The **Bodyguard dies instead** and the target
     lives (a Doctor heal on the Bodyguard saves them too).
   - Is the target healed by a Doctor? They survive.
   - Otherwise, the target dies.
4. **Investigations** — Detective and Consigliere get their results. An
   investigator who died tonight gets nothing.

A Bodyguard can only intercept one attack per night.

## Host Settings

| Setting | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Night timer** | How long night lasts. It ends early once everyone with a night action has acted. "No limit" waits for everyone. |
| **Discussion timer** | How long the town talks before voting. The host can always cut it short with "End discussion." |
| **Voting timer** | How long the vote stays open. It closes early once every living player has voted. |
| **Reveal role on death** | On: dead players' roles are shown to everyone. Off: deaths are anonymous — much harder for the town. |
| **First-night kills** | Off: night 1 is a truce — nobody can die, but investigations still happen. Gentler for new groups. |
| **Role setup** | Use the suggested distribution (it explains its reasoning and adapts to your player count), or adjust any role's count yourself. The game warns you about rough balance but won't stop you — it's your party. |

## Voting Details

- Votes are public — everyone sees who voted for whom.
- A revealed **Mayor's vote counts twice**, including a vote to Skip.
- Most votes wins. If Skip gets at least as many votes as the leading player,
  or the leaders tie, nobody is executed.
- The vote ends as soon as every living player has voted.

## Disconnections

Losing your connection doesn't kill you immediately: you have about 90
seconds to reload the page and you'll land right back in your seat, with the
full event log restored. Stay gone longer and you're eliminated as having
abandoned the town.
